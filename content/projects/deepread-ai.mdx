<section className="bespoke-section">
  <div className="bespoke-section-label">01 — The Observation</div>
  <h2 className="bespoke-h2">The problem wasn't attention spans. It was a broken format.</h2>
  <p className="bespoke-p">I was using Gemini's deep research feature one afternoon. Instead of reading the usual wall of text, it handed me something different — an interactive HTML page. Collapsible sections. Embedded charts. A 3,000-word report compressed into a focused 5-minute read.</p>
  <p className="bespoke-p">I finished it. That almost never happens.</p>

  <div className="bespoke-pull-quote">
    <p>"People haven't stopped wanting to learn. They've stopped tolerating formats that don't respect their time."</p>
  </div>

  <p className="bespoke-p">Blog readership is declining, but it's misdiagnosed. Publishers keep blaming TikTok and short attention spans. The real culprit is a format that was designed for desktop reading in 2008 and hasn't changed since. <strong>The format is the failure mode — not the reader.</strong></p>
</section>

<section className="bespoke-section">
  <div className="bespoke-section-label">02 — The Thinking</div>
  <h2 className="bespoke-h2">I did PM in reverse — and it worked.</h2>
  <p className="bespoke-p">Most product thinking goes: find a problem → build a solution. I went the other direction, and I'd do it again.</p>

  <div className="bespoke-insight">
    <div className="bespoke-insight-label">The Method</div>
    <p>I had a working solution — a format that demonstrably changed how people consumed information. So I reverse-engineered the problem it solved, then validated that the problem was real for others, not just me.</p>
  </div>

  <p className="bespoke-p">I sent the Gemini-style interactive blog to a small group of people. No pitch, no framing. I just watched whether they finished reading. <strong>They did.</strong> That's a more honest signal than any survey.</p>
  <p className="bespoke-p">Small validation cohorts beat large indifferent ones. What matters isn't sample size — it's whether the behavior you're betting on actually happened.</p>
</section>

<section className="bespoke-section">
  <div className="bespoke-section-label">03 — The Strategy</div>
  <h2 className="bespoke-h2">Three niches. One audience. A monetization architecture.</h2>
  <p className="bespoke-p">Niche selection is a capital allocation decision. Get it wrong and you don't just grow slowly — you eliminate the business case. I chose <strong>AI & Technology, Finance, and Health</strong> — not because they're trending, but because of two specific things:</p>
  <p className="bespoke-p">First, the same person reads all three. The 24-year-old comparing LLM tools on Monday is researching index funds on Thursday and tracking macros on the weekend. That's one reader, three touch points, one platform.</p>
  <p className="bespoke-p">Second — and this is the one most people miss — Finance and Health carry some of the highest advertising CPMs on the open web. <strong>The niche selection is also the monetization architecture.</strong></p>

  <div className="bespoke-metrics">
    <div className="bespoke-metric">
      <div className="bespoke-metric-value">3×</div>
      <div className="bespoke-metric-label">Audience cross-overlap</div>
      <div className="bespoke-metric-sub">Same reader, all 3 verticals</div>
    </div>
    <div className="bespoke-metric">
      <div className="bespoke-metric-value">2–3 min</div>
      <div className="bespoke-metric-label">Target read time</div>
      <div className="bespoke-metric-sub">vs. 10–15 min industry avg</div>
    </div>
    <div className="bespoke-metric">
      <div className="bespoke-metric-value">Top 3</div>
      <div className="bespoke-metric-label">CPM verticals chosen</div>
      <div className="bespoke-metric-sub">Finance · Health · Tech</div>
    </div>
    <div className="bespoke-metric">
      <div className="bespoke-metric-value">Day 1</div>
      <div className="bespoke-metric-label">Newsletter launched</div>
      <div className="bespoke-metric-sub">Owned audience from the start</div>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

<section className="bespoke-section">
  <div className="bespoke-section-label">04 — Decisions & Trade-offs</div>
  <h2 className="bespoke-h2">Every decision had a cost. Here's what I accepted.</h2>
  <p className="bespoke-p">Good product decisions aren't just about what you build — they're about what you knowingly give up. These were mine.</p>

  <div className="bespoke-decisions">
    <div className="bespoke-decision bespoke-decision-header">
      <div className="bespoke-decision-what">Decision</div>
      <div className="bespoke-decision-why">Why I accepted the trade-off</div>
    </div>
    <div className="bespoke-decision">
      <div className="bespoke-decision-what">200–500 word articles</div>
      <div className="bespoke-decision-why">Completion rate over depth. A finished short article beats an abandoned long one every time.</div>
    </div>
    <div className="bespoke-decision">
      <div className="bespoke-decision-what">Launch multi-niche, not single</div>
      <div className="bespoke-decision-why">Slower to own one category — but faster to grow total audience and reach monetization threshold.</div>
    </div>
    <div className="bespoke-decision">
      <div className="bespoke-decision-what">Newsletter from day zero</div>
      <div className="bespoke-decision-why">Traffic is borrowed. An email list is owned. I wanted an audience I couldn't lose to an algorithm update.</div>
    </div>
    <div className="bespoke-decision">
      <div className="bespoke-decision-what">Format as the moat</div>
      <div className="bespoke-decision-why">Content can be copied. A format that readers genuinely prefer is much harder to replicate at scale.</div>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

<section className="bespoke-section">
  <div className="bespoke-section-label">05 — What I Learned</div>
  <h2 className="bespoke-h2">Four things this build made permanent in how I think.</h2>

  <ul className="bespoke-learnings">
    <li>
      <span className="bespoke-learning-num">01</span>
      <div className="bespoke-learning-text">
        <strong>Format is a product decision</strong>
        How information is presented determines whether it's consumed at all. Most content products treat format as a design afterthought. It should be the first PM question.
      </div>
    </li>
    <li>
      <span className="bespoke-learning-num">02</span>
      <div className="bespoke-learning-text">
        <strong>Validation doesn't need scale — it needs honesty</strong>
        A handful of people with no incentive to flatter you is worth more than a thousand survey responses. Watch the behavior. The truth is in whether they finished.
      </div>
    </li>
    <li>
      <span className="bespoke-learning-num">03</span>
      <div className="bespoke-learning-text">
        <strong>Revenue architecture should be designed at day one</strong>
        Monetization retrofitted later is a product rebuilt from scratch. Every early decision — niche, format, email capture — was made with revenue pathway in mind.
      </div>
    </li>
    <li>
      <span className="bespoke-learning-num">04</span>
      <div className="bespoke-learning-text">
        <strong>Observed behavior is a faster path to insight than user interviews</strong>
        When a format demonstrably changes how real people consume information, the problem it solves is real — even before anyone has articulated it.
      </div>
    </li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section className="bespoke-section">

  <h2 className="bespoke-h2">A blog is the front door. The ecosystem is the business.</h2>
  <p className="bespoke-p">Cognition is built to be more than a content platform. The engaged reader base it builds becomes the distribution layer for everything else — a PM interview prep tool, a habit tracker, personalized AI-powered summaries on demand.</p>

  <div className="bespoke-pull-quote">
    <p>"The content is the top of funnel. The ecosystem is the business."</p>

  <p className="bespoke-p">Every project I build from here has a home to launch from. That was the plan from the beginning.</p>
