01 — The Observation

The problem wasn't attention spans. It was a broken format.

I was using Gemini's deep research feature one afternoon. Instead of reading the usual wall of text, it handed me something different — an interactive HTML page. Collapsible sections. Embedded charts. A 3,000-word report compressed into a focused 5-minute read.

I finished it. That almost never happens.

"People haven't stopped wanting to learn. They've stopped tolerating formats that don't respect their time."

Blog readership is declining, but it's misdiagnosed. Publishers keep blaming TikTok and short attention spans. The real culprit is a format that was designed for desktop reading in 2008 and hasn't changed since. The format is the failure mode — not the reader.

02 — The Thinking

I did PM in reverse — and it worked.

Most product thinking goes: find a problem → build a solution. I went the other direction, and I'd do it again.

The Method

I had a working solution — a format that demonstrably changed how people consumed information. So I reverse-engineered the problem it solved, then validated that the problem was real for others, not just me.

I sent the Gemini-style interactive blog to a small group of people. No pitch, no framing. I just watched whether they finished reading. They did. That's a more honest signal than any survey.

Small validation cohorts beat large indifferent ones. What matters isn't sample size — it's whether the behavior you're betting on actually happened.

03 — The Strategy

Three niches. One audience. A monetization architecture.

Niche selection is a capital allocation decision. Get it wrong and you don't just grow slowly — you eliminate the business case. I chose AI & Technology, Finance, and Health — not because they're trending, but because of two specific things:

First, the same person reads all three. The 24-year-old comparing LLM tools on Monday is researching index funds on Thursday and tracking macros on the weekend. That's one reader, three touch points, one platform.

Second — and this is the one most people miss — Finance and Health carry some of the highest advertising CPMs on the open web. The niche selection is also the monetization architecture.

04 — Decisions & Trade-offs

Every decision had a cost. Here's what I accepted.

Good product decisions aren't just about what you build — they're about what you knowingly give up. These were mine.

Decision
Why I accepted the trade-off
200–500 word articles
Completion rate over depth. A finished short article beats an abandoned long one every time.
Launch multi-niche, not single
Slower to own one category — but faster to grow total audience and reach monetization threshold.
Newsletter from day zero
Traffic is borrowed. An email list is owned. I wanted an audience I couldn't lose to an algorithm update.
Format as the moat
Content can be copied. A format that readers genuinely prefer is much harder to replicate at scale.
05 — What I Learned

Four things this build made permanent in how I think.

  • 01
    Format is a product decision How information is presented determines whether it's consumed at all. Most content products treat format as a design afterthought. It should be the first PM question.
  • 02
    Validation doesn't need scale — it needs honesty A handful of people with no incentive to flatter you is worth more than a thousand survey responses. Watch the behavior. The truth is in whether they finished.
  • 03
    Revenue architecture should be designed at day one Monetization retrofitted later is a product rebuilt from scratch. Every early decision — niche, format, email capture — was made with revenue pathway in mind.
  • 04
    Observed behavior is a faster path to insight than user interviews When a format demonstrably changes how real people consume information, the problem it solves is real — even before anyone has articulated it.
06 — What's Next

A blog is the front door. The ecosystem is the business.

Cognition is built to be more than a content platform. The engaged reader base it builds becomes the distribution layer for everything else — a PM interview prep tool, a habit tracker, personalized AI-powered summaries on demand.

"The content is the top of funnel. The ecosystem is the business."

Every project I build from here has a home to launch from. That was the plan from the beginning.
